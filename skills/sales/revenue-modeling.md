# Skill: Revenue Model Analysis

## Purpose
Map a company's revenue model, pricing structure, and
expansion levers from their public pricing page and signals.

## Pricing Model Classification

### By Structure
- **Flat-rate**: One price for everything (simple, low expansion)
- **Seat/User-based**: Price × number of users (common in B2B SaaS)
- **Usage-based**: Price per API call / transaction / volume (high expansion potential)
- **Tiered**: Feature gates across 2-5 tiers (most common)
- **Module-based**: Core + add-ons (enterprise expansion play)
- **Custom/Enterprise**: No public pricing (high ACV, long sales cycle)
- **Freemium**: Free base, paid premium (PLG conversion play)

### Expansion Revenue Levers (from pricing page)
- More seats / users
- Higher usage limits (API calls, storage, records)
- Unlocking premium features (SSO, advanced analytics, priority support)
- Additional modules or integrations
- Professional services / onboarding fees

### Price Points to Record
```
Tier 1 (Starter):  $[X]/month [per seat?] — [Y users, Z features]
Tier 2 (Pro):      $[X]/month [per seat?] — [Y users, Z features]
Tier 3 (Business): $[X]/month [per seat?] — [Y users, Z features]
Tier 4 (Enterprise): Contact sales
Annual discount: [X]% if visible
```

## ACV Estimation
Average Contract Value estimate based on pricing + ICP:
- SMB: Starter × 3-10 seats × 12 months
- Mid-Market: Pro/Business × 10-100 seats × 12 months
- Enterprise: Custom × estimated negotiated rate

## Cohort Expansion Model
Based on tier structure, estimate:
- Typical entry-level ACV
- Typical expanded ACV at year 2
- Net Revenue Retention estimate (high NRR = usage-based or seat-based)

## Output
```

Model Type: [flat/seat/usage/tiered/custom]

```
