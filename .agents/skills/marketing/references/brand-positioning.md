# Skill: Brand Positioning Assessment

## Purpose
Evaluate the clarity, strength, and differentiation of
a company's brand positioning from their public presence.

## Positioning Extraction Framework

### Step 1: Extract the Positioning Statement
From the homepage H1, hero section, and About page:
- What is the ONE sentence that describes what they do and for whom?
- Does the positioning communicate differentiated value?
- Is it benefit-led or feature-led?

Rate clarity: Unclear / Vague / Clear / Compelling

### Step 2: Test the "5-Second Test"
Can you answer these in 5 seconds from the homepage?
- [ ] What does this company do?
- [ ] Who is it for?
- [ ] Why is it better than alternatives?
- [ ] What is the next step (CTA)?

### Step 3: Differentiation Analysis
Search for how they describe their differentiation:
```
site:{company_domain} "only" OR "first" OR "unlike" OR "better than" OR "vs"
```
What unique claims do they make?
- Technology claims ("the only AI-powered...")
- Speed/performance claims ("10x faster than...")
- Ease of use claims ("no code required...")
- Integration claims ("connects with everything you use...")
- Price claims ("fraction of the cost of...")

Assess: Are these claims credible and supported? Or empty marketing?

### Step 4: Tone of Voice Analysis
Review homepage, blog, and social:
- Formal / Professional
- Friendly / Conversational
- Technical / Developer-focused
- Bold / Challenger brand
- Trustworthy / Enterprise-safe

Is the tone consistent across channels?

### Step 5: Social Proof Quality
- Number of testimonials visible
- Specificity of testimonials (vague praise vs. ROI metrics)
- Named logos visible on homepage
- Review site badges (G2, Capterra)
- Press logo bar

Rate social proof: Weak / Moderate / Strong / Exceptional

## Output
```
**Brand Positioning Assessment — [Company Name]**

Positioning Clarity: [Unclear / Vague / Clear / Compelling]
5-Second Test: [Passes / Partial / Fails]
Differentiation Claims: [list]
Tone of Voice: [descriptor]
Tone Consistency: [Inconsistent / Mostly Consistent / Highly Consistent]
Social Proof Strength: [Weak / Moderate / Strong / Exceptional]

Positioning Strengths:
- [strength]

Positioning Weaknesses:
- [weakness]

Recommended Positioning Improvements:
1. [specific change]
```
